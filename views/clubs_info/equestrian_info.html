About Our Club

The Oriental Club at Al-Kalamoon University is a club that aims to preserve the oriental cultural heritage and promote a deeper understanding of traditional oriental cultures. The club organizes events that celebrate oriental music, literature, arts, and customs and traditions that represent the oriental identity. The club provides students with the opportunity to learn oriental skills and arts such as playing traditional musical instruments such as the oud and qanun, and oriental folkloric dances. The club also encourages discussion and dialogue sessions about oriental heritage and the challenges it faces in light of globalization. In addition, the club organizes cultural evenings and festivals that celebrate oriental heritage, allowing students the opportunity to learn about their heritage and engage in activities that highlight the aesthetics of oriental civilization.

Our Goals

  • Preserving the Oriental Heritage: Promoting the Oriental cultural identity among students by introducing and preserving the Oriental heritage, customs and traditions.
  • Teaching the Oriental Arts: Organizing workshops and lessons to teach traditional Oriental musical arts such as the oud, qanun, and nay, in addition to folkloric dances.
  • Spreading awareness of the Oriental culture: Organizing cultural events and evenings that focus on literature, music and Oriental arts to promote a deeper understanding of these cultures among students.
  • Encouraging dialogue about heritage: Creating a platform for discussion and dialogue about issues related to the Oriental heritage and the challenges facing its preservation in light of modern developments.
